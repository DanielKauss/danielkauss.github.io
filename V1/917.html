Riding on Urushi’s back, she ran through Sendia. Her legs were much stronger than I had imagined, climbing up the walls of the building and running through it as fast as I could.

But the results were not looking good.

Naturally, our first destination was the tower. That’s when we tried to ask the soldiers for help, but......

Most of the soldiers had just arrived at the gates. Moreover, Seliadot was nowhere to be found.

The Chief Soldier, who was in command, looked at me and twisted his face. He clearly thought I was in his way.

No matter how much we asked him, he wouldn’t let us send our soldiers. He’ll protect this tower, won’t the other forces trust us? What was he saying when this town was about to be destroyed?

Even if they directly appealed to the soldiers still in the tower, they would not move because they were ordered by the Chief Soldier. They would probably only leave someone under their command in the tower.

I wondered what they were doing, but they had been evacuating a variety of valuables. Money, ornaments, medicine, and other supplies were packed into evacuation item bags and brought out.

Money in this critical situation? How stupid. We couldn’t rely on this as soon as possible.

At the very least, our salvation would be that there were very few patients left. Seliadot must have ordered us to evacuate.

I gave up the support from the tower and decided to go somewhere else. Mea-san and Velmeria-san must have been working together for the Beastmen Union and the Dragon King Syndicate. Then I decided to go to the Adventurers Guild next. That’s what I thought...

There were very few people in the guild. Many adventurers had already moved to the defense of the gate.

There were quite a few antidemons rushing over to them, so they couldn’t afford to reduce their numbers. They also said that the remaining adventurers could not reduce them to protect the guild.

Once a city was destroyed, they had nothing to do with the organization...... But this was no different here?

When she shouted further that she needed troops, a man named the submaster yelled at her. Apparently, he had prepared to escape from the city. He had apparently left behind some adventurers under his command as his escort.

How could they be the only ones who would try to escape at a time like this...? I couldn’t rely on the Adventurers Guild either. Then I thought they would cooperate with a small organization......
